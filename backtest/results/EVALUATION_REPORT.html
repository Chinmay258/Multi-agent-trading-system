Limitations & caveats

  • No edge demonstrated. Strategy returns are near zero and indistinguishable from random entries with matched frequency. Beating buy & hold here is mostly an artifact of low exposure during a falling market, not predictive skill.
  • Conservative 2% sizing caps both risk and reward, so absolute PnL is small by design — judge the strategy on Sharpe / win rate / vs-random, not on dollar return.
  • Weak classifier. ~33% is random on 3 classes; the model lands ~0.41–0.49 and the HOLD class is essentially unlearned. Directional precision is near coin-flip.
  • Limited history. Bundled sample data is modest; results are indicative, not a multi-year, multi-regime validation. Single asset, single timeframe.
  • Exit model is explicit. Paper mode has no native SL/TP, so the backtest applies a documented SL/TP bracket; live paper behaviour can differ.
  • No survivorship/regime adjustments and no slippage modelling beyond a flat per-side fraction. Real fills on thin books would be worse.
